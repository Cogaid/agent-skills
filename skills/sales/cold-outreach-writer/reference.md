## Personalization Framework

### The 3x3 Research Method

Spend 3 minutes finding 3 relevant facts:
1. Something about the **person**
2. Something about the **company**
3. Something that **connects** them to your solution

### Personalization Tiers

**Tier 1 - Basic (30 seconds):**
- First name
- Company name
- Role/title

**Tier 2 - Standard (3 minutes):**
- Recent company news
- LinkedIn profile details
- Industry context

**Tier 3 - Deep (15 minutes):**
- Content they've created
- Specific initiatives
- Mutual connections
- Personal interests
- Quote from interview

### Example Progressions

**Generic:**
"I help companies improve their sales."

**Basic:**
"I help tech companies like [Company] improve their sales."

**Standard:**
"Saw [Company] just raised Series B - congrats! Many fast-growing SaaS companies struggle to scale sales at this stage."

**Deep:**
"Loved your recent post about scaling outbound at [Company]. Your point about personalization resonated - we've found the same thing at [My Company]. Curious if you've explored [specific tactic]?"

## Email Copywriting

### Opening Lines That Work

| Type | Example | Why It Works |
|------|---------|--------------|
| Observation | "Noticed you're hiring 5 SDRs" | Shows research |
| Compliment | "Your post on [topic] was spot on" | Genuine flattery |
| Mutual | "[Name] suggested I reach out" | Trust transfer |
| Challenge | "Most [role] struggle with [problem]" | Identifies pain |
| Question | "How are you handling [challenge]?" | Engages thinking |
| News | "Saw the [news/announcement]" | Timely relevance |

### Value Propositions

**Structure:**
We help [target audience] [achieve outcome] by [method] so they can [benefit].

**Examples:**
- "We help sales teams book 3x more meetings by automating personalized outreach, so they can focus on closing deals."
- "We help e-commerce brands reduce cart abandonment by 40% through AI-powered email sequences."
- "We help engineering teams ship 2x faster by automating code reviews and testing."

### Call-to-Action Options

| CTA Type | Example | When to Use |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| Meeting | "Worth a 15-min chat?" | Direct ask |
| Question | "Is this a priority for you?" | Soft approach |
| Resource | "Mind if I send our guide?" | Value-first |
| Referral | "Who should I talk to about this?" | Wrong contact |
| Yes/No | "Interested?" | Very busy prospects |

## Technical Best Practices

### Email Deliverability

| Factor | Best Practice |
|--------|---------------|
| Sender reputation | Warm up new domains |
| SPF/DKIM/DMARC | Configure properly |
| Sending volume | Start slow, increase gradually |
| Bounce rate | Keep under 2% |
| Spam complaints | Keep under 0.1% |
| Unsubscribe | Include visible link |

### Spam Triggers to Avoid

**Words:**
- Free, guarantee, urgent, act now
- $$, make money, limited time
- Click here, buy now, order now
- Congratulations, winner

**Formatting:**
- ALL CAPS
- Excessive exclamation marks!!!
- Red text
- Large images with little text
- Broken links

### Optimal Send Times

| Day | Best Time | Why |
|-----|-----------|-----|
| Tuesday | 8-10 AM | Start of productive week |
| Wednesday | 8-10 AM | Mid-week focus |
| Thursday | 8-10 AM | Before weekend planning |
| Avoid | Monday AM, Friday PM | Catching up / winding down |

## Sequence Strategy

### Multi-Touch Framework

```
Day 0: Email 1 (Initial outreach)
Day 1: LinkedIn view profile
Day 2: LinkedIn connection request
Day 3: Email 2 (New angle)
Day 5: LinkedIn comment on their post
Day 7: Email 3 (Social proof)
Day 10: Phone call attempt
Day 14: Email 4 (Breakup)
Day 30: Email 5 (Re-engagement)
```

### Channel Mixing

| Channel | Purpose | Timing |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Email | Primary outreach | Throughout |
| LinkedIn | Supplement + research | Between emails |
| Phone | High-value prospects | After engagement |
| Video | Stand out | Replace Email 2 or 3 |

## A/B Testing

### What to Test

| Element | Variables | Priority |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Subject line | Length, personalization, emoji | High |
| Opening line | Personalization level, angle | High |
| CTA | Direct vs. soft, specific vs. open | Medium |
| Email length | Short vs. medium | Medium |
| Send time | Time of day, day of week | Low |
| Signature | Photo, title, links | Low |

### Test Methodology

1. **Sample size**: Minimum 100 per variant
2. **Duration**: Run for at least 1 week
3. **One variable**: Change only one thing
4. **Statistical significance**: Wait for 95%+ confidence
5. **Document**: Record all tests and results

## Metrics & Tracking

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Benchmark |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Open rate | Opens / Delivered | 20-40% |
| Reply rate | Replies / Delivered | 2-10% |
| Positive reply rate | Positive replies / All replies | 30-50% |
| Meeting rate | Meetings / Delivered | 1-5% |
| Conversion rate | Meetings / Positive replies | 50-70% |

## Objection Handles

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Not interested" | "Totally understand. Mind sharing what's working for you currently?" |
| "Send info" | "Happy to! What specifically would be most helpful?" |
| "Bad timing" | "No problem. When would be better to reconnect?" |
| "Using competitor" | "Makes sense! How's that going? Curious what made you choose them." |
| "No budget" | "Understood. Worth a quick chat anyway in case timing changes?" |
| "Talk to [other person]" | "Thanks! Would you be open to connecting us?" |
