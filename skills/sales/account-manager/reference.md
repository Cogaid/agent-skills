# Account Manager Reference Guide

## Health Score Deep Dive

### Calculating Health Score

```
Health Score = (Usage × 0.30) + (Engagement × 0.25) +
               (Support × 0.20) + (Relationship × 0.15) +
               (Business Impact × 0.10)

Score Range: 0-100
```

### Component Scoring

**Usage Score (0-100):**
| Metric | Weight | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|--------|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| DAU/MAU ratio | 40% | >30% | 15-30% | <15% |
| Feature adoption | 30% | >60% features | 30-60% | <30% |
| Trend | 30% | Growing | Flat | Declining |

**Engagement Score (0-100):**
| Metric | Weight | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|--------|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| Meeting attendance | 35% | >80% | 50-80% | <50% |
| Email response rate | 35% | <24hr | 24-72hr | >72hr |
| NPS/CSAT | 30% | >8 | 6-8 | <6 |

**Support Score (0-100):**
| Metric | Weight | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|--------|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| Ticket volume trend | 40% | Decreasing | Stable | Increasing |
| Resolution satisfaction | 40% | >90% | 70-90% | <70% |
| Escalation rate | 20% | <5% | 5-15% | >15% |

**Relationship Score (0-100):**
| Metric | Weight | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|--------|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| Stakeholder access | 40% | Exec + users | Manager | Single contact |
| Sentiment | 30% | Positive | Neutral | Negative |
| Multi-threading | 30% | 3+ contacts | 2 contacts | 1 contact |

**Business Impact Score (0-100):**
| Metric | Weight | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|--------|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| ROI achieved | 50% | >100% | 50-100% | <50% |
| Goals met | 30% | >80% | 50-80% | <50% |
| Value articulation | 20% | Clear | Partial | Unclear |

## Expansion Revenue Strategies

### Land and Expand Model

```
Phase 1: LAND
- Initial department/team
- Core use case
- Prove value (90 days)

Phase 2: EXPAND - DEPTH
- Additional users
- Premium features
- Advanced use cases

Phase 3: EXPAND - BREADTH
- Other departments
- New use cases
- Enterprise license

Phase 4: STRATEGIC
- Company-wide rollout
- Multi-product
- Strategic partnership
```

### Expansion Conversation Framework

**The Triple Play:**
1. **Usage-Based**: "You're at 85% of your limit..."
2. **Value-Based**: "You've achieved X, imagine doing Y..."
3. **Strategic-Based**: "Given your priority of Z..."

### Identifying Expansion Signals

| Signal Type | Specific Indicators | Expansion Type |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------|
| Usage | Approaching limits | Volume upsell |
| Behavior | Power user patterns | Tier upgrade |
| Verbal | Feature requests | Add-on products |
| Organizational | New teams, growth | Additional seats |
| Strategic | New initiatives | Cross-sell |
| Timing | Contract anniversary | Multi-year |

## Renewal Management

### Renewal Stages

```
120 Days Out: HEALTH CHECK
- Review health score
- Address any risks
- Begin re-engagement if needed

90 Days Out: KICKOFF
- Schedule renewal discussion
- Share value summary
- Identify expansion potential

60 Days Out: PROPOSAL
- Present renewal options
- Include expansion recommendations
- Share pricing

30 Days Out: NEGOTIATION
- Handle objections
- Finalize terms
- Prepare contract

14 Days Out: CLOSE
- Obtain signatures
- Process internally
- Confirm next steps

Day 0: RENEWAL
- Send confirmation
- Set success plan for new term
- Celebrate with customer
```

### Renewal Pricing Strategies

| Scenario | Pricing Approach | Rationale |
|----------|------------------|-----------|
| Healthy, growing | Price increase (3-5%) | Value delivered |
| Healthy, stable | Hold pricing | Retain |
| At-risk | Discount or hold | Save the customer |
| Expanding | Bundle discount | Reward growth |
| Multi-year | Annual discount | Lock in |

### Renewal Risk Indicators

| Risk Factor | Weight | Red Flag |
|-------------|--------|----------|
| Champion leaving | High | Within 90 days of renewal |
| Usage declining | High | >20% drop |
| Support issues | Medium | Unresolved escalation |
| Budget concerns | Medium | Mentioned in last quarter |
| Competitor evaluation | High | Active comparison |

## QBR Best Practices

### QBR Types

| Type | Frequency | Attendees | Duration |
|------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Executive QBR | Quarterly | C-suite | 60 min |
| Business QBR | Quarterly | Managers | 45 min |
| Technical QBR | Monthly | Power users | 30 min |
| Value Check | Ad-hoc | Any | 15 min |

### QBR Deck Template

```
1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (1 slide)
   - Key metrics
   - Achievements
   - Focus areas

2. VALUE DELIVERED (2-3 slides)
   - ROI achieved
   - Goals accomplished
   - Time/cost savings

3. USAGE INSIGHTS (2 slides)
   - Adoption metrics
   - Feature usage
   - Benchmarks vs. peers

4. SUCCESS STORIES (1 slide)
   - Wins from their team
   - Similar customer examples

5. ROADMAP (2 slides)
   - Upcoming features
   - Beta opportunities
   - Their feature requests

6. PARTNERSHIP PLAN (1-2 slides)
   - Next quarter goals
   - Action items
   - Expansion opportunities

7. APPENDIX
   - Detailed metrics
   - Support history
   - Training resources
```

### QBR Preparation Checklist

```
1 Week Before:
□ Pull usage data
□ Review support history
□ Check business news
□ Draft deck
□ Identify expansion angles

2 Days Before:
□ Send agenda to customer
□ Confirm attendees
□ Finalize deck
□ Prepare questions

Day Of:
□ Test technology
□ Have backup plan
□ Bring insights, not just data
```
