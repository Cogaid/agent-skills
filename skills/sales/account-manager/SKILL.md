## Purpose

Grow and retain customer accounts through proactive relationship management, value delivery, and strategic expansion.

## Quick Reference

### Account Management KPIs

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Net Revenue Retention | >110% | (Start + Expansion - Churn) / Start |
| Gross Retention | >90% | (Start - Churn) / Start |
| Expansion Rate | >30% | Expansion Revenue / Start |
| Customer Health Score | >80 | Composite score |
| NPS | >50 | Promoters - Detractors |

### Account Tiers

| Tier | ARR Range | Touch Model | QBR Frequency |
|------|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Enterprise | $100K+ | High-touch | Monthly |
| Mid-Market | $25K-$100K | Medium-touch | Quarterly |
| SMB | $5K-$25K | Tech-touch + periodic | Bi-annually |
| Self-Serve | <$5K | Automated | On-demand |

## Account Health Framework

### Health Score Components

| Factor | Weight | Indicators |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Product Usage | 30% | DAU/MAU, feature adoption |
| Engagement | 25% | Response rate, meeting attendance |
| Support Health | 20% | Ticket volume, satisfaction |
| Relationship | 15% | Stakeholder access, sentiment |
| Business Impact | 10% | ROI achieved, goals met |

### Health Score Levels

| Score | Status | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 80-100 | Healthy | Expansion focus |
| 60-79 | Monitor | Proactive check-in |
| 40-59 | At Risk | Intervention needed |
| 0-39 | Critical | Executive escalation |

## Workflow

### 1. Quarterly Business Review (QBR)

```
QBR Agenda (60 min):

1. REVIEW (15 min)
   - Usage metrics and trends
   - Goals achieved vs. targets
   - Support history

2. VALUE (15 min)
   - ROI demonstration
   - Success stories
   - Best practices shared

3. ROADMAP (15 min)
   - Product updates
   - Upcoming features
   - Beta opportunities

4. STRATEGY (15 min)
   - Their business priorities
   - Expansion opportunities
   - Action items
```

### 2. Regular Touchpoint Cadence

| Account Tier | Check-in | QBR | Executive Sponsor |
|--------------|----------|-----|-------------------|
| Enterprise | Weekly | Monthly | Quarterly |
| Mid-Market | Bi-weekly | Quarterly | Bi-annually |
| SMB | Monthly | Bi-annually | Annually |

### 3. Expansion Identification

```
Expansion Signals:
□ Usage approaching limits
□ New use cases mentioned
□ Additional teams interested
□ Budget cycle timing
□ Organizational growth
□ New initiatives aligning with product
□ Champion promotion
□ Competitor displacement
```

## Expansion Playbook

### Upsell Opportunities

| Trigger | Upsell Motion | Talk Track |
|---------|---------------|------------|
| Usage at 80%+ | More seats/capacity | "You're getting great adoption..." |
| Power user emergence | Premium tier | "Your team is using advanced features..." |
| Feature requests | Add-on products | "I noticed you asked about..." |
| Contract anniversary | Multi-year | "Lock in current pricing..." |

### Cross-Sell Opportunities

| Signal | Cross-Sell | Approach |
|--------|------------|----------|
| Adjacent team interest | Additional product | Intro meeting |
| Process gaps mentioned | Complementary solution | Discovery call |
| Executive priority shift | New product line | Strategic review |

### Expansion Conversation Template

```
"[Name], based on [observation/data], it looks like [insight].

Many customers at this stage start [using additional capability/product].

[Social proof from similar customer]

Would it make sense to explore how [expansion opportunity]
could help [their specific goal]?"
```

## Renewal Management

### Renewal Timeline

| Time Before | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| 120 days | Health check, flag risks |
| 90 days | Renewal kickoff meeting |
| 60 days | Proposal and terms |
| 30 days | Contract negotiation |
| 14 days | Signature chase |
| 0 days | Renewal or churn |

### Renewal Conversation Framework

```
1. VALUE RECAP
   "Over the past year, you've [achievements with our product]..."

2. FUTURE VISION
   "Looking ahead, here's what we're building that aligns
   with [their priorities]..."

3. RENEWAL TERMS
   "For renewal, I'd recommend [option] because..."

4. EXPANSION OPPORTUNITY
   "Given [signal], you might also benefit from [upsell]..."

5. COMMITMENT
   "Can we get this wrapped up by [date]?"
```

### Handling Renewal Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Not using it enough" | Usage analysis + reactivation plan |
| "Too expensive" | ROI review + optimization |
| "Considering alternatives" | Competitive differentiation |
| "Budget cuts" | Downgrade options, payment terms |
| "Stakeholder left" | New champion development |

## Risk Management

### Early Warning Signs

| Indicator | Risk Level | Action |
|-----------|------------|--------|
| Usage drop >20% | High | Same-day outreach |
| No login 14+ days | Medium | Check-in email |
| Support complaints | Medium | Root cause analysis |
| Champion departure | High | New relationship building |
| Missed meetings | Low | Alternative touchpoint |
| Negative feedback | High | Executive escalation |

### Risk Mitigation Playbook

**Usage Decline:**
```
1. Identify drop timing and cause
2. Schedule check-in call
3. Offer retraining/onboarding
4. Create success plan with milestones
5. Weekly monitoring for 30 days
```

**Champion Departure:**
```
1. Thank outgoing champion
2. Request new contact introduction
3. Offer onboarding to new contact
4. Share institutional knowledge
5. Build multi-threaded relationships
```

**Support Escalation:**
```
1. Join support ticket
2. Own communication
3. Provide regular updates
4. Post-resolution follow-up
5. Document lessons learned
```

## Best Practices

### Do's
- Know your accounts inside and out
- Be proactive, not reactive
- Multi-thread relationships
- Document everything in CRM
- Celebrate customer wins
- Ask for referrals when healthy
- Provide value every interaction

### Don'ts
- Wait for problems to escalate
- Single-thread relationships
- Only reach out for renewals
- Ignore usage data
- Over-promise
- Forget the human element
