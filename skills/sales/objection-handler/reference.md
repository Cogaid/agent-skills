## Objection Categories

### Price Objections

**Signals**:
- "It's too expensive"
- "We don't have the budget"
- "Your competitor is cheaper"
- "Can you give us a discount?"

**Root Causes**:
- Haven't established value yet
- Comparing to wrong alternative
- Budget genuinely limited
- Testing for discount

**Strategy**: Reframe to value, not cost

---

### Timing Objections

**Signals**:
- "Not the right time"
- "We're too busy"
- "Call me next quarter"
- "We have other priorities"

**Root Causes**:
- Genuine competing priority
- Procrastination/fear of change
- Polite brush-off
- Need more urgency

**Strategy**: Quantify cost of delay

---

### Authority Objections

**Signals**:
- "I need to run this by my boss"
- "The committee decides"
- "I'm not the decision maker"

**Root Causes**:
- Multi-stakeholder process
- Champion needs ammunition
- Risk aversion

**Strategy**: Enable the champion

---

### Need Objections

**Signals**:
- "We're fine with what we have"
- "Don't see why we'd change"
- "Not a priority"

**Root Causes**:
- Pain not established
- Status quo bias
- Lack of awareness

**Strategy**: Go back to discovery

---

### Trust Objections

**Signals**:
- "Never heard of you"
- "How do we know this works?"
- "What if it doesn't work out?"

**Root Causes**:
- Risk aversion
- Past bad experience
- Need social proof

**Strategy**: Proof points and guarantees

---

### Competition Objections

**Signals**:
- "We're looking at [Competitor]"
- "Why should we choose you?"
- "[Competitor] has feature X"

**Root Causes**:
- Doing due diligence
- Leverage for negotiation
- Genuine evaluation

**Strategy**: Differentiate on value

## LAER Framework Deep Dive

### L - Listen

**What to do**:
- Let them finish completely
- Take notes on exact words
- Watch for emotion
- Don't formulate response while they talk

**What NOT to do**:
- Interrupt
- Get defensive
- Dismiss the concern
- Jump to solution

---

### A - Acknowledge

**Purpose**: Make them feel heard

**Phrases that work**:
- "I appreciate you sharing that..."
- "That's a fair concern..."
- "I understand why you'd feel that way..."
- "You're not the first to raise that..."

**Phrases to AVOID**:
- "But..." (negates acknowledgment)
- "Actually..." (sounds defensive)
- "You're wrong about that..."
- "That's not true..."

---

### E - Explore

**Purpose**: Understand root cause

**Questions to ask**:
- "Help me understand what's behind that..."
- "When you say [X], what do you mean specifically?"
- "Is that the only concern, or are there others?"
- "What would need to be true for this to make sense?"
- "Have you faced this before? What happened?"

**What you're looking for**:
- Real vs. stated objection
- Logical vs. emotional concern
- Solvable vs. fundamental blocker

---

### R - Respond

**Only after understanding**:
1. Address the specific concern (not generic pitch)
2. Use evidence (case study, data, testimonial)
3. Check if resolved: "Does that address your concern?"
4. Advance: "What would you like to do next?"

## Response Strategies by Type

### Price Objections

**"It's too expensive"**

Strategy: Reframe the comparison

```
"I understand it feels like a significant investment. Can I ask -
what are you comparing us to? [pause for answer]

The real comparison isn't us vs. [alternative], it's the cost of
solving this problem vs. the cost of not solving it.

You mentioned [problem] is costing you [quantified impact]. Our
solution typically delivers [X] return. Does the math work?"
```

**"Your competitor is cheaper"**

Strategy: Differentiate on value

```
"They might be. Can I ask what you're comparing specifically?
[pause]

We often see [Competitor] looks cheaper initially, but customers
tell us [hidden cost/limitation]. Here's a case study from [Similar
Company] who made that comparison...

What matters most to you beyond price?"
```

---

### Timing Objections

**"Not the right time"**

Strategy: Quantify delay cost

```
"I understand - timing matters. Can I ask what's driving that?
[pause for answer]

Here's what I'm thinking about: You mentioned [problem] is costing
[X per month]. If we wait 3 months, that's [X * 3] you're leaving
on the table.

What would need to happen to move this up the priority list?"
```

**"Call me next quarter"**

Strategy: Create smaller commitment

```
"Happy to follow up then. Before I do - what will be different
next quarter? [pause]

Sometimes the best time to start is during a slower period so
you're ready when things pick up. What if we did a smaller
pilot now so you're set up for [next quarter]?"
```

---

### Authority Objections

**"I need to check with my boss"**

Strategy: Enable the champion

```
"Absolutely - what do you think they'll want to know? [pause]

How about this: I'll put together a one-pager with the key points
and ROI case. Would it help if I joined that conversation to
answer questions directly?

What's the best way to set that up?"
```

---

### Competition Objections

**"We're also looking at [Competitor]"**

Strategy: Understand and differentiate

```
"Smart to evaluate options. What do you like about them so far?
[pause - take notes]

Here's where we typically differ: [2-3 key differentiators].

The customers who choose us over [Competitor] usually care most
about [key value]. Is that important to you?"
```

## Objection Prevention

### During Discovery

- Uncover budget early: "Do you have budget allocated?"
- Identify decision makers: "Who else is involved?"
- Establish timeline: "When do you need this solved?"
- Build urgency: "What happens if you don't solve this?"

### During Presentation

- Preempt common objections: "You might be wondering about..."
- Use social proof: "Customers like you typically..."
- Set expectations: "The investment is..."
- Confirm at each step: "Does this make sense so far?"

### Before Close

- Trial close: "How are you feeling about this?"
- Surface concerns: "What questions do you still have?"
- Get commitment: "What would you need to move forward?"
