# Objection Response Templates

## Price Objections

### "Too Expensive"

```
"I appreciate you being direct about that. Help me understand -
when you say expensive, what are you comparing us to?

[After they answer]

That's helpful. Here's how I think about it: [Problem you discussed]
is currently costing you [quantified impact]. Our customers typically
see [ROI timeframe and amount].

If we can deliver [specific outcome], would the investment make sense?"
```

### "Over Budget"

```
"Budget is real - I get it. A few options we could explore:

1. Start smaller: We could pilot with [subset] and expand later
2. Phase the investment: Split across [timeframes]
3. Find budget: Sometimes [problem] costs come from other budgets

Which of these might work for your situation?"
```

### "Competitor is Cheaper"

```
"They might be - and I appreciate you telling me. Two questions:

1. What specifically are you comparing? Sometimes the packages differ
2. What's most important to you beyond price?

[After they answer]

Here's where we differ: [2-3 differentiators relevant to their answers].
Customers tell us the [specific value] is worth the premium. Here's a
case study from [Similar Company]..."
```

### "Need a Discount"

```
"I want to work with you on this. Help me understand what would make
the difference - is it the total investment or the terms?

[If total] What number would work for you, and why?
[If terms] Would spreading payments over [X months] help?

Let me see what I can do and get back to you by [time]."
```

## Timing Objections

### "Not the Right Time"

```
"I understand - timing matters. What's making now difficult?

[After they answer]

That makes sense. Here's what I'm weighing: You mentioned [problem]
costs [X/month]. Waiting [timeframe] means [total cost].

What if we could start with something small that doesn't add to your
current load? Then you'd be set up when things ease up."
```

### "Too Busy Right Now"

```
"I hear you - it sounds intense. The customers I work with often say
the same thing, then realize [solution] actually saves them time.

What if we looked at this differently: What's taking up most of your
time right now?

[After they answer]

Interesting - because [solution] specifically helps with [relevant benefit].
What would it be worth to get [X hours] back each week?"
```

### "Call Me Next Quarter"

```
"Happy to do that. Before I go - what will be different then?

[After they answer]

Got it. Here's my concern: [Problem] doesn't pause while we wait.
If it's costing [X/month], that's [X * 3] between now and then.

What if we at least got you set up now at current pricing, and you
can start when ready? That way you're not scrambling later."
```

## Authority Objections

### "Need to Run This By My Boss"

```
"Absolutely - that makes sense. A few questions to help me help you:

1. What do you think they'll want to know?
2. What concerns might they have?
3. Have they seen solutions like this before?

How about I put together a one-pager with the ROI case and key points?
Would it help if I joined that conversation?"
```

### "Committee Decision"

```
"Got it - how does that process typically work?

[After they answer]

That's helpful. Based on what you've shared, here's what I think
each stakeholder will care about:

- [Role 1]: [Their priority]
- [Role 2]: [Their priority]

I can put together materials for each. Would it help to present
directly to the group?"
```

### "Not My Decision"

```
"I appreciate you being upfront. Help me understand your role in
this - are you evaluating options for someone else?

[After they answer]

Perfect. What would make you confident recommending us? What would
[decision maker] need to see?

Let's make sure you have everything you need to be the hero here."
```

## Need/Status Quo Objections

### "We're Fine With What We Have"

```
"That's fair - if it's working, why change? Can I ask a few questions?

1. What's working well about your current approach?
2. If you could wave a magic wand, what would you improve?
3. How has [relevant metric] changed over the past year?

[After they answer]

Interesting. The reason I ask is that customers like [Similar Company]
said the same thing, until they realized [hidden cost/limitation].
Does any of that resonate?"
```

### "Not a Priority"

```
"I understand - you're focused on what matters most. What are your
top priorities right now?

[After they answer]

That makes sense. Here's why this came up: Companies focused on
[their priority] often find [your solution] actually accelerates that.

[Similar Company] was in the same boat - they prioritized [thing],
then realized [connection to your solution]. Worth exploring?"
```

## Trust Objections

### "Never Heard of You"

```
"That's fair - we're [size/stage]. Here's why that might actually
be good for you:

1. We're hungry to prove ourselves
2. You get white-glove attention, not a number
3. Our [key metric: customers/growth/team] shows we're on to something

Here are some companies like yours who took a chance: [references].
Would it help to talk to one of them directly?"
```

### "How Do I Know This Works?"

```
"Great question - you shouldn't just take my word for it. Here's
what I can offer:

1. Case studies from companies like yours: [Examples]
2. References you can call directly
3. [Trial/pilot/POC] so you can see it yourself
4. [Guarantee] if we don't deliver

Which would be most helpful for you?"
```

### "What If It Doesn't Work?"

```
"Smart to think about that. A few things:

1. Here's our track record: [success rate/metrics]
2. Here's what implementation looks like: [process]
3. Here's our guarantee: [specific guarantee]
4. Here's what support looks like: [support model]

What specifically are you most concerned about?"
```

## Competition Objections

### "Looking at Competitor Too"

```
"Smart to evaluate options. Can I ask - what do you like about them
so far?

[After they answer]

Got it. Here's how I see the key differences:

| Area | Us | Them |
|------|-----|------|
| [Key area 1] | [Our strength] | [Their gap] |
| [Key area 2] | [Our strength] | [Their gap] |
| [Key area 3] | [Our strength] | [Their gap] |

The customers who choose us typically care most about [differentiator].
How important is that to you?"
```

### "Competitor Has Feature X"

```
"You're right - they do have that. Can I ask how you'd use [Feature X]?

[After they answer]

That makes sense. Here's our perspective: We deliberately [didn't build
it / built it differently] because [reasoning]. Our customers tell us
[alternative approach] actually works better because [reason].

Would it help to talk to a customer who evaluated both?"
```

---

## Usage Tips

1. **Pause after their objection** - Let silence work for you
2. **Take notes on exact words** - Use their language back
3. **Explore before responding** - Make sure you understand
4. **Check resolution** - "Does that address your concern?"
5. **Advance** - Always suggest a next step
