# Negotiation Handler Reference

## Negotiation Psychology

### Cognitive Biases in Negotiation

| Bias | Description | How to Use |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Anchoring | First number sets range | Anchor high |
| Loss aversion | Losses feel 2x worse than gains | Frame as avoiding loss |
| Reciprocity | Feel obligated to return favors | Give small things first |
| Social proof | Follow what others do | Share customer stories |
| Scarcity | Rare = valuable | Limited availability |
| Authority | Trust experts | Share expertise |

### Emotional Triggers

| Emotion | Trigger | Response |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Fear | Risk of wrong decision | Reduce risk, add guarantees |
| Pride | Being seen as smart | Make them feel smart |
| Anxiety | Uncertainty | Provide clarity, social proof |
| Excitement | Vision of success | Paint future picture |
| Frustration | Obstacles | Remove friction |

## BATNA Analysis

### Understanding BATNA

**BATNA** = Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement

**Your BATNA:**
- What happens if this deal doesn't close?
- Other pipeline opportunities?
- Recurring revenue from existing customers?
- Market demand for your product?

**Their BATNA:**
- What are their alternatives?
- Cost of doing nothing?
- Competitor options?
- In-house solution?
- Time and switching costs?

### BATNA Comparison Matrix

| Factor | Your BATNA | Their BATNA |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Alternatives | List options | Research competitors |
| Time pressure | Quota deadline? | Implementation deadline? |
| Consequences | Revenue impact | Problem persists |
| Switching cost | Sales effort | Implementation effort |

### Strengthening Your Position

1. **Improve your BATNA**
   - Strong pipeline reduces desperation
   - Multiple deals in flight
   - Healthy business regardless

2. **Weaken their BATNA**
   - Show competitor weaknesses
   - Demonstrate unique value
   - Create urgency around timing

## Pricing Psychology

### Value-Based Pricing

```
Traditional: Cost + Margin = Price
Value-Based: Value to Customer × % = Price

Example:
- Your tool saves 10 hours/week
- At $50/hour = $500/week value
- $2,000/month = 10% of value
- Easy ROI justification
```

### Price Presentation

| Technique | Example | Effect |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| Annual vs. monthly | "$99/month" vs "$1,188/year" | Monthly seems smaller |
| Per user/seat | "$15/user/month" | Scales naturally |
| Compared to cost | "Less than one hire" | Relative value |
| ROI framing | "3x return in 6 months" | Investment mindset |

### Discount Impact Math

| Scenario | List Price | Discount | Revenue Impact |
|----------|-----------|----------|----------------|
| Standard | $100,000 | 0% | $100,000 |
| 10% discount | $100,000 | 10% | $90,000 |
| 20% discount | $100,000 | 20% | $80,000 |
| 30% discount | $100,000 | 30% | $70,000 |

**At 70% margin:**
- 10% discount = 33% more volume needed
- 20% discount = 60% more volume needed
- 30% discount = 100% more volume needed

## Deal Structures

### Payment Terms

| Term | Description | When to Offer |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Annual prepaid | Full year upfront | Discount justified |
| Quarterly | Every 3 months | Balance for both |
| Monthly | Every month | Higher total price |
| Net 30 | Pay within 30 days | Standard B2B |
| Net 60/90 | Pay within 60/90 days | Enterprise, trade for value |

### Contract Lengths

| Length | Discount Range | Best For |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| Month-to-month | None | Low commitment |
| Annual | 0-10% | Standard |
| 2-year | 10-20% | Growth accounts |
| 3-year | 15-25% | Strategic accounts |
| 5-year | 20-30% | Enterprise |

### Phased Deals

**Phase 1 - Pilot:**
- Limited scope/users
- Short timeframe (60-90 days)
- Clear success criteria
- Auto-rollout clause

**Phase 2 - Expansion:**
- Full deployment
- Multi-year commitment
- Volume pricing
- Partnership terms

## Handling Specific Situations

### The Budget Objection

```
Pattern: "Your price is above our budget"

Questions to Ask:
1. "What is the budget?" (Get specific number)
2. "Is this set or can it be adjusted for ROI?"
3. "What's included in that budget?"
4. "When does the fiscal year end?"
5. "Is there budget in other departments?"

Responses:
- "What if we phased implementation?"
- "Could we start with a smaller scope?"
- "What if we did quarterly payments?"
- "Can we align start date with next budget cycle?"
```

### The Competitor Comparison

```
Pattern: "Competitor X is 30% cheaper"

Questions to Ask:
1. "What specifically are they including?"
2. "Have you spoken to their customers?"
3. "What's their implementation approach?"
4. "How do they handle [key differentiator]?"

Reframe:
"The upfront price is one factor. Let's look at total cost:
- Implementation time/cost
- Training and adoption
- Ongoing support
- Results timeline
- Risk of wrong choice

Our customers find the total investment actually lower."
```

### The "I Need to Think About It"

```
Pattern: Stalling or cold feet

Diagnose:
1. "What specifically needs more thought?"
2. "What would make this an easy yes?"
3. "What concerns haven't we addressed?"
4. "Is there anyone else who needs to weigh in?"

If Real Concern:
- Address the specific issue
- Offer additional information
- Connect with reference customer

If Stalling:
- Create genuine urgency
- Offer decision assistance
- Set clear follow-up
```

### The Last-Minute Ask (Nibbling)

```
Pattern: "Just one more thing..." after agreement

Examples:
- "Can you throw in free training?"
- "Can we add 5 more users?"
- "Can you extend payment terms?"

Response Framework:
1. Acknowledge: "I understand you'd like X"
2. Restate: "We've agreed on [terms]"
3. Trade: "If I include X, can we finalize today?"
4. Hold: "I've given you my best offer on pricing -
    I can't add more without adjusting something else"
```

## Closing Techniques

### Trial Close

Test readiness without asking for commitment:
- "How does this look so far?"
- "Does this address your main concern?"
- "Are we moving in the right direction?"

### Summary Close

Recap agreements to build momentum:
- "So we've agreed on [X], [Y], and [Z]"
- "The only remaining item is [specific issue]"
- "Assuming we resolve that, are we good to go?"

### Assumptive Close

Proceed as if they've decided:
- "Let's talk about implementation timing"
- "I'll send over the contract this afternoon"
- "Who should I copy on the agreement?"

### Alternative Close

Give choices, not yes/no:
- "Would you prefer to start this month or next?"
- "Do you want the annual or quarterly plan?"
- "Should we include the premium support?"

### Urgency Close

Create legitimate time pressure:
- "Pricing increases next month"
- "Implementation slots are filling up"
- "This quarter's promotion ends Friday"

## Negotiation Metrics

### Deal Analysis

| Metric | Formula | Healthy Range |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Discount rate | Discount / List price | <15% average |
| Win rate | Deals won / Deals negotiated | >30% |
| Negotiation cycle | Days from first ask to close | <14 days |
| Multi-year rate | Multi-year / Total deals | >40% |
| Expansion rate | Upsells / Total customers | >20% |

### Discount Distribution

| Discount Level | Target % of Deals |
|----------------|------------------|
| 0% (list price) | 20-30% |
| 1-10% | 40-50% |
| 11-20% | 15-25% |
| 21-30% | 5-10% |
| 30%+ | <5% |

## Concession Strategy

### Rules for Concessions

1. **Never concede first on price**
2. **Make concessions smaller over time**
3. **Always get something in return**
4. **Make them work for concessions**
5. **Document concessions as valuable**

### Concession Pattern

```
❌ Bad: "We can do $100K" → "$80K" → "$60K"
   (Large jumps, signals more room)

✓ Good: "We can do $100K" → "$95K" → "$92K"
   (Smaller, shows reaching limit)
```

### Non-Price Concessions

| Concession | Value to Them | Cost to You |
|------------|---------------|-------------|
| Extended trial | Medium | Low |
| Free training | High | Medium |
| Premium support | High | Medium |
| Early access | Medium | Low |
| Extra users | Medium | Low |
| Custom report | Medium | High |

## Legal/Contract Considerations

### Common Contract Points

| Item | Standard | Negotiable |
|------|----------|------------|
| Payment terms | Net 30 | Yes - for commitment |
| Auto-renewal | Yes | Sometimes |
| Price lock | 1 year | Multi-year = lock |
| SLA | Standard | Enterprise only |
| Liability cap | Contract value | Case by case |
| Termination | 30 days notice | Longer possible |
| Data ownership | Customer owns | Usually fixed |

### Red Flags in Negotiations

- Extremely aggressive timeline
- Unusual payment structures
- Extensive liability demands
- Won't involve legal team
- Reluctance to document
- Last-minute stakeholder changes
