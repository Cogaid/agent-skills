## Writing Guidelines

### Voice & Tone

**Do**:
- Use their words (from discovery notes)
- Write in second person ("you" not "the customer")
- Be specific with numbers and outcomes
- Keep sentences short
- Use active voice

**Don't**:
- Use jargon they haven't used
- Write long paragraphs
- Make unsubstantiated claims
- Focus on features over outcomes
- Use filler words

### Formatting Best Practices

- **Headers**: Clear, scannable hierarchy
- **Bullets**: For lists of 3+ items
- **Tables**: For comparisons and pricing
- **Bold**: For key numbers and takeaways
- **Whitespace**: Generous margins and spacing

## Section Deep Dives

### Executive Summary

The most important section - many executives only read this.

**Structure** (1 page max):
1. Their situation (2-3 sentences using their words)
2. What they want (1-2 sentences)
3. How you'll help (2-3 sentences)
4. Expected outcomes (2-3 bullets, quantified)
5. Investment & timeline (brief)

**Example Opening**:
```
[Company] is currently [specific challenge from discovery],
resulting in [quantified impact they mentioned].

Your goal is to [outcome they stated] by [their timeline].
```

**Example Close**:
```
Investment: $X - $Y depending on [scope variable]
Timeline: [Duration] to full deployment
Expected ROI: [X]% in first year based on [similar customer]
```

---

### Current Situation

**Purpose**: Prove you understand their world.

**Include**:
- Direct quotes from discovery
- Specific numbers they shared
- Current process/tools mentioned
- Why now is the right time

**Avoid**:
- Generic industry challenges
- Assumptions not confirmed
- Problems they didn't mention

---

### Desired Outcomes

**Purpose**: Align on success definition.

**Format**:
```
Primary Objective:
[Specific goal with measurable target]

Success Metrics:
| Metric | Current | Target | Timeline |
|--------|---------|--------|----------|
| [Metric 1] | [X] | [Y] | [When] |
| [Metric 2] | [X] | [Y] | [When] |

Key Requirements:
• [Must-have 1]
• [Must-have 2]
```

---

### Proposed Solution

**Structure**:
1. **Approach overview**: How you solve their problem
2. **Scope of work**: Specific deliverables with phases
3. **What's included**: Features, services, support
4. **What's not included**: Boundary setting

**Scope Format**:
```
Phase 1: [Name] ([Duration])
• [Deliverable 1]
• [Deliverable 2]
→ Milestone: [What marks completion]

Phase 2: [Name] ([Duration])
• [Deliverable 1]
• [Deliverable 2]
→ Milestone: [What marks completion]
```

---

### Why Us

**Include**:
- Relevant experience (similar customers)
- Specific differentiators for their situation
- Customer quotes/testimonials
- Risk mitigation (guarantees, support)

**Avoid**:
- Generic "we're the best" claims
- Irrelevant features
- Competitor bashing

**Proof Point Format**:
```
"[Quote about specific outcome]"
— [Name], [Title], [Company]

Result: [Quantified outcome]
```

---

### Investment

**Never bury the price** - put it clearly.

**Single Price Format**:
```
Total Investment: $[Amount]

Includes:
• [Item 1]: $[Amount]
• [Item 2]: $[Amount]

Payment Terms: [Terms]
```

**Tiered Options Format**:
```
| Component | Essential | Pro | Enterprise |
|-----------|-----------|-----|------------|
| [Feature] | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ |
| [Feature] | - | ✓ | ✓ |
| Investment | $X | $Y | $Z |

Recommended: [Option] because [reason for this prospect]
```

---

### Value Justification

Always include ROI framing:

```
Current Cost of Problem:
• [Cost 1]: $[Amount/year]
• [Cost 2]: $[Amount/year]
Total: $[Amount/year]

Expected Return:
• [Benefit 1]: $[Amount]
• [Benefit 2]: $[Amount]
Projected Benefit: $[Amount/year]

Investment: $[Amount]
Payback Period: [Timeframe]
Year 1 ROI: [Percentage]
```

---

### Timeline

**Format**:
```
Week 1-2: [Phase Name]
• [Activity 1]
• [Activity 2]
→ Deliverable: [What they receive]

Week 3-4: [Phase Name]
• [Activity 1]
• [Activity 2]
→ Deliverable: [What they receive]

First Value Delivery: [Date]
Full Implementation: [Date]

Assumptions:
• [What you need from them]
• [Resource availability]
```

### Next Steps

**Make it easy**:
```
To proceed:
1. [ ] Review and provide feedback by [Date]
2. [ ] Schedule kick-off call: [Link to calendar]
3. [ ] Sign agreement: [Link or attachment]

Upon agreement:
• Day 1: [First activity]
• Week 1: [First milestone]

Questions? [Name] at [Email] or [Phone]
```

## Review Checklist

### Before Sending

**Content**:
- [ ] Addresses their specific situation (not generic)
- [ ] Uses their words from discovery
- [ ] ROI is clear and believable
- [ ] Case studies are relevant to them
- [ ] Scope is clear (inclusions and exclusions)
- [ ] Timeline is realistic

**Pricing**:
- [ ] Easy to understand
- [ ] Value justification included
- [ ] Options if appropriate
- [ ] Payment terms clear

**Format**:
- [ ] Company name correct throughout
- [ ] Contact names spelled correctly
- [ ] Numbers accurate
- [ ] Professional design
- [ ] No typos
- [ ] 5-8 pages total

**Next Steps**:
- [ ] Crystal clear what to do
- [ ] Specific dates included
- [ ] Easy to accept

## Follow-Up Strategy

### Day of Sending
- Send with brief email highlighting 2-3 key points
- Offer to walk through together
- Confirm receipt

### 2-3 Days After
```
Hi [Name],

Just checking in - did you get a chance to review the proposal?

Happy to walk through it together or answer any questions.

[Your name]
```

### 1 Week After
```
Hi [Name],

Following up on the proposal I sent last week.

I know [their priority] is important to you. If there are
any questions or concerns, I'd love to address them.

What would be most helpful at this point?

[Your name]
```

### 2 Weeks After
```
Hi [Name],

Wanted to check in on timing. Based on our conversation,
you wanted to [achieve goal] by [their timeline].

To hit that timeline, we'd need to kick off by [date].

Is this still a priority? If anything has changed, let me know
so I can adjust the proposal accordingly.

[Your name]
```

## Common Mistakes

1. **Generic executive summary** - Use their specific situation
2. **Too much about you** - Focus on their outcomes
3. **Burying the price** - Be upfront about investment
4. **No clear ROI** - Quantify the value
5. **Vague next steps** - Be specific about what happens next
6. **Too long** - 5-8 pages is ideal
7. **Wrong names/numbers** - Always double-check
