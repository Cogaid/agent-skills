## Sequence Psychology

### The Follow-Up Mindset

**Why Multiple Touches Work:**
- 80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups
- Most reps give up after 2 attempts
- Persistence (done right) signals value
- Different messages resonate at different times

### Prospect Psychology by Stage

**Initial Outreach:**
- They don't know you
- They're busy
- Default is ignore
- Need compelling reason to respond

**After Touch 2-3:**
- Some name recognition
- May be evaluating relevance
- Testing if you'll go away
- Need proof of value

**After Touch 4-5:**
- Pattern recognition kicks in
- Either annoyed or impressed
- Ready to engage or block
- Breakup message can trigger response

## Timing & Cadence

### Optimal Send Times

| Day | Best Time | Avoid |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| Monday | 10-11am | Before 9am |
| Tuesday | 8-10am, 4-5pm | Lunch hour |
| Wednesday | 8-10am, 4-5pm | Lunch hour |
| Thursday | 8-10am, 2-3pm | Late afternoon |
| Friday | 8-9am only | After noon |

### Spacing Between Touches

**Cold Outreach:**
```
Touch 1 → (2-3 days) → Touch 2 → (3-4 days) → Touch 3
Touch 3 → (5-7 days) → Touch 4 → (4-5 days) → Touch 5
Touch 5 → (7-10 days) → Touch 6 (Breakup)
```

**Warm Follow-Up (Post-Meeting):**
```
Meeting → (same day thank you) → Touch 1
Touch 1 → (2 days) → Touch 2
Touch 2 → (3-4 days) → Touch 3
Touch 3 → (5-7 days) → Touch 4 (Breakup)
```

### Holiday/Event Adjustments

- **Major holidays:** Pause 2 days before, 1 day after
- **Industry conferences:** Pause during, restart after
- **Company announcements:** Pause during big news
- **Quarterly end:** Expect slower response rates

## Channel Strategy

### Channel Selection by Persona

| Role | Primary | Secondary | Avoid |
|------|---------|-----------|-------|
| C-Suite | LinkedIn, Email | Phone | Text |
| VP | Email, Phone | LinkedIn | Cold call |
| Director | Email | LinkedIn, Phone | - |
| Manager | Email, Phone | LinkedIn | - |
| Individual | Email | LinkedIn | Cold call |

### Multi-Channel Sequence Pattern

```
Day 1: Email #1
Day 2: LinkedIn connection + note
Day 4: Email #2
Day 7: Phone call + voicemail
Day 9: LinkedIn message
Day 12: Email #3 (case study)
Day 15: Phone call
Day 18: Email #4 (breakup)
```

### Channel-Specific Tips

**Email:**
- Short subject lines (4-7 words)
- Mobile-optimized
- Single CTA
- PS lines get read

**LinkedIn:**
- Connect before messaging
- Reference mutual connections
- Share relevant content
- Don't pitch immediately

**Phone:**
- Best for decision-makers
- Have voicemail script ready
- Reference previous emails
- Call twice (AM and PM)

## Personalization Techniques

### Levels of Personalization

**Level 1: Basic (Minimum)**
```
- First name
- Company name
- Title
- Industry
```

**Level 2: Research-Based**
```
- Recent company news
- Job tenure
- Company growth stage
- Tech stack signals
```

**Level 3: Deep Personalization**
```
- Personal interests (LinkedIn activity)
- Content they've published
- Mutual connections
- Conference attendance
- Podcast/interview quotes
```

### Personalization Triggers

**Company Signals:**
- Funding announcement → Scale/growth messaging
- New hire in your buyer area → Timing opportunity
- Expansion news → Resource needs
- Restructuring → Change management pain

**Personal Signals:**
- Job change → New initiative opportunity
- Posted about a challenge → Direct solution
- Engaged with competitor → Comparison angle
- Published content → Thought leadership connection

### The 3x3 Research Framework

Spend 3 minutes finding 3 things:

1. **Recent trigger** (company or personal news)
2. **Common ground** (mutual connection, school, location)
3. **Relevant observation** (content, company direction)

Use 1 per email to show genuine interest.

## A/B Testing

### What to Test

**Subject Lines:**
- Question vs. statement
- Name inclusion vs. not
- Numbers vs. no numbers
- Length (short vs. medium)

**Email Body:**
- Opening hook
- Pain point emphasis
- Proof point type
- CTA phrasing

**Sequence Level:**
- Number of touches
- Days between touches
- Channel mix
- Content types

### Testing Framework

```
1. Define hypothesis
2. Create A and B variants
3. Split audience randomly
4. Run for statistical significance (100+ per variant)
5. Measure primary metric (usually reply rate)
6. Implement winner
7. Test next element
```

### Sample Size Calculator

| Response Rate | Min Sample per Variant |
|---------------|------------------------|
| 1-2% | 2,500 |
| 3-5% | 800 |
| 6-10% | 400 |
| 10-15% | 200 |

## Sequence Templates by Scenario

### Cold Outreach to Enterprise

**Touch 1: The Opener**
- Reference company trigger
- Propose relevant outcome
- One question CTA

**Touch 2: The Value Add**
- Share relevant content (not yours)
- Position as helpful
- No pitch

**Touch 3: The Social Proof**
- Similar company success
- Specific metrics
- Soft CTA

**Touch 4: The Different Angle**
- New value proposition
- Different pain point
- Multiple response options

**Touch 5: The Direct Ask**
- Acknowledge persistence
- Clear meeting request
- Limited time options

**Touch 6: The Breakup**
- Respect their time
- Open door for future
- Often highest response

### Re-Engagement Sequence

**Touch 1: The Check-In**
```
Subject: [Their priority] update?

Hi [Name],

We last spoke about [specific topic] a few months ago.
Your team was exploring [initiative].

Curious if that's still on the radar or if
priorities have shifted?

Either way, no pressure - just wanted to check in.

[Signature]
```

**Touch 2: New Value**
```
Subject: New approach to [their challenge]

[Name],

Since we last connected, we've helped [similar company]
achieve [specific result].

The approach was different from what we discussed -
happy to share what worked for them.

Worth 15 minutes?

[Signature]
```

**Touch 3: Content Share**
```
Subject: Thought of you

[Name],

Just published this [content type] on [topic relevant to them].

Given your focus on [their area], thought it
might be useful.

Here's the link: [URL]

Let me know if you want to discuss any of it.

[Signature]
```

## Response Handling

### When They Reply Positively

1. Respond within 1 hour (business hours)
2. Confirm understanding of their interest
3. Propose specific next step
4. Include calendar link
5. Stop automation

### When They Reply Negatively

**"Not interested":**
- Thank them for response
- Ask what's not relevant
- Offer to reconnect later
- Remove from sequence

**"Not the right person":**
- Ask for correct contact
- Thank them
- Add referral to sequence

**"Timing is off":**
- Acknowledge timing
- Ask when to reconnect
- Set reminder
- Send relevant content periodically

### When They Go Silent

- Complete sequence
- Add to low-priority nurture
- Re-engage in 3-6 months
- Trigger on company news

## Compliance & Best Practices

### CAN-SPAM Compliance

- Clear sender identification
- Accurate subject lines
- Include physical address
- Easy unsubscribe option
- Honor opt-outs promptly

### GDPR Considerations

- Legitimate interest basis
- Clear about data usage
- Right to erasure
- Record consent status

### Professional Standards

- Never misrepresent yourself
- Don't use false urgency
- Respect unsubscribe requests
- Be truthful about capabilities
