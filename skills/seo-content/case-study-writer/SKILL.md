## Purpose

Create case studies that tell persuasive stories of customer success to build credibility and drive conversions.

## Quick Reference

### Case Study Lengths

| Format | Length | Use Case |
|--------|--------|----------|
| One-pager | 300-500 words | Sales collateral |
| Standard | 800-1200 words | Website, marketing |
| Long-form | 1500-2500 words | SEO, deep dive |
| Video script | 2-3 minutes | Video production |

### Essential Elements

| Element | Purpose | Priority |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Customer profile | Establish relatability | Critical |
| Challenge | Create tension | Critical |
| Solution | Show your product | Critical |
| Results | Prove value | Critical |
| Quote | Add authenticity | High |
| Metrics | Quantify success | High |

## Case Study Framework (SCQA+R)

### Structure

| Section | Content | Length |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **S**ituation | Customer background, context | 10-15% |
| **C**hallenge | Problems they faced | 15-20% |
| **Q**uest | Evaluation and decision | 10-15% |
| **A**ction | How they used your product | 20-25% |
| **R**esults | Outcomes and metrics | 25-30% |

## Interview Guide

### Questions to Ask

```
Background:
1. Tell me about [Company] and your role.
2. What were you using before [our product]?

Challenge:
3. What problems were you trying to solve?
4. How did this impact your team/business?
5. What did you try before finding us?

Solution:
6. How did you find [our product]?
7. Why did you choose us over alternatives?
8. How was the implementation process?

Results:
9. What results have you achieved?
10. Can you share specific numbers?
11. What surprised you most?
12. How has it changed your work?

Close:
13. What would you tell someone considering us?
14. What's next for your team with [our product]?
```

### Getting Good Quotes

Ask for quotes that:
- Include specific numbers
- Express emotion
- Address common objections
- Are attributable

**Prompt:** "If someone was on the fence about [product], what would you tell them?"

## Best Practices

### Do
- Lead with results
- Use specific numbers
- Include direct quotes
- Tell a story arc
- Make customer the hero
- Show before/after
- Address objections implicitly

### Don't
- Focus too much on your product
- Use vague results ("improved efficiency")
- Write without customer approval
- Forget the CTA
- Make it too long
- Skip the challenge section

## Approval Process

```
1. Interview customer
2. Draft case study
3. Internal review (marketing, sales)
4. Send to customer for fact-check
5. Incorporate feedback
6. Get written approval
7. Publish and promote
```
