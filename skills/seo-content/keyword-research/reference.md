## Keyword Difficulty Assessment

### Factors That Increase Difficulty
- High domain authority competitors
- Big brands dominating SERP
- Established, comprehensive content
- High backlink counts on ranking pages
- Featured snippets, knowledge panels

### Factors That Decrease Difficulty
- Forums, Q&A sites ranking (content gap)
- Thin or outdated content ranking
- Low authority sites ranking
- New or trending topics

### Quick Difficulty Check
1. Search the keyword in Google
2. Look at top 5 results
3. Ask: "Can I create something significantly better?"
4. Check: Are any low-authority sites ranking?
5. Assess: How strong are backlink profiles?

## Clustering Strategy

### Cluster Structure

```
PILLAR TOPIC: [Main topic]
│
├── Cluster 1: [Subtopic]
│   ├── [Supporting keyword 1]
│   ├── [Supporting keyword 2]
│   └── [Supporting keyword 3]
│
├── Cluster 2: [Subtopic]
│   ├── [Supporting keyword 1]
│   ├── [Supporting keyword 2]
│   └── [Supporting keyword 3]
│
└── Cluster 3: [Subtopic]
    ├── [Supporting keyword 1]
    └── [Supporting keyword 2]
```

### Clustering Rules
- Pillar page targets broad, competitive term
- Cluster pages target specific long-tail variations
- All cluster pages link to pillar page
- Pillar page links to all cluster pages
- Internal linking strengthens topical relevance

## Prioritization Framework

### Scoring Model (1-5 each)

| Factor | 1 | 5 |
|--------|---|---|
| Business Value | Unrelated | Direct revenue |
| Search Volume | Very low | High |
| Ranking Potential | Very difficult | Easy |
| Content Fit | Doesn't match expertise | Perfect match |
| Conversion Potential | Pure info | Clear path |

**Priority Score** = (Business Value × 2) + Volume + Ranking + Fit + Conversion

### Priority Matrix

```
                HIGH VOLUME

    ┌───────────────┼───────────────┐
```
