## Expense Policies

### Reimbursable Expenses

**Travel:**
- Economy class airfare
- Standard hotel rooms
- Ground transportation (taxi, rideshare, rental)
- Parking and tolls
- Travel insurance (if required)

**Meals:**
- Client entertainment (with documentation)
- Team meals during travel
- Working lunches (with business purpose)
- Conference/event meals

**Office & Equipment:**
- Work-related supplies
- Computer equipment (with approval)
- Software subscriptions
- Home office setup (with policy)

**Professional Development:**
- Conference registration
- Training courses
- Professional memberships
- Books and publications

### Non-Reimbursable Expenses

- Personal entertainment
- Alcohol (unless client entertainment)
- Personal travel upgrades
- Gym memberships
- Personal phone plans
- Traffic violations/tickets
- Lost or stolen items

### Expense Limits

| Category | Daily Limit | Approval Required |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Meals (solo) | $50 | No |
| Meals (client) | $150/person | > $150 total |
| Hotel | $200/night | > $200 |
| Airfare | Economy | Business class |
| Equipment | $500 | > $500 |

## Tax Deduction Guide

### Fully Deductible (100%)

**Business Travel:**
- Transportation to client/work sites
- Lodging while traveling
- Business-related calls
- Baggage fees

**Office Expenses:**
- Office supplies
- Software subscriptions
- Professional services
- Business insurance

**Professional Development:**
- Industry conferences
- Relevant training
- Professional certifications
- Trade publications

### Partially Deductible (50%)

**Meals and Entertainment:**
- Business meals with clients
- Team meals with clear business purpose
- Client entertainment

**Home Office:**
- Portion based on square footage
- Internet (business percentage)
- Phone (business percentage)

### Documentation Required

| Expense Type | Required Documentation |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Travel | Receipt + itinerary + business purpose |
| Meals > $75 | Receipt + attendees + business purpose |
| Meals < $75 | Receipt + business purpose |
| Equipment | Receipt + business use justification |
| Mileage | Log with date, destination, purpose |

## Receipt Management

### What to Capture

**Essential Information:**
- Vendor name
- Date of purchase
- Total amount (including tax)
- Payment method
- Itemized list (for meals)

**For Meals:**
- Names of attendees
- Business purpose discussed
- Company/organization of guests

### Receipt Storage

**Digital Best Practices:**
1. Photo immediately after purchase
2. Use consistent naming: `YYYY-MM-DD_Vendor_Amount.jpg`
3. Store in organized folders by month
4. Backup to cloud storage
5. Keep originals for audit period (7 years)

### No Receipt? What to Do

1. Request duplicate from vendor
2. Use bank/card statement as backup
3. Write detailed memo of purchase
4. Get supervisor approval
5. Note in expense report

## Expense Categories Detailed

### Travel

**Airfare:**
- Book economy unless flight > 6 hours
- Compare prices from multiple sources
- Book 2+ weeks in advance when possible

**Hotels:**
- Standard room in mid-range hotel
- Book through approved channels
- Include taxes and fees in total

**Ground Transportation:**
- Public transit when practical
- Rideshare/taxi when necessary
- Rental car when cost-effective

**Mileage:**
- Current IRS rate: $0.67/mile (2024)
- Log all business trips
- Include starting/ending locations

### Meals

**Business Meals:**
- Must have clear business purpose
- Record all attendees
- Keep itemized receipt
- Tip included in total (reasonable %)

**Per Diem Option:**
- Flat daily rate instead of receipts
- Varies by location
- Check current GSA rates

### Office Supplies

**Typical Items:**
- Notebooks, pens, paper
- Printer supplies
- Desk organization
- Shipping/postage

**Equipment:**
- Requires approval > $500
- Must be for business use
- May need asset tagging

## Reporting Guidelines

### Expense Report Structure

```
Header:
- Report name/number
- Employee name
- Department
- Date range
- Total amount

Body:
- Line items by date
- Category per item
- Receipts attached

Footer:
- Certification statement
- Signature
- Approval signatures
```

### Submission Timeline

| Expense Type | Submit Within |
|--------------|---------------|
| Travel | 10 business days of return |
| Monthly recurring | End of month |
| One-time purchases | 30 days |
| Annual subscriptions | At renewal |

### Approval Chain

1. **< $500:** Manager approval
2. **$500 - $2,000:** Director approval
3. **> $2,000:** VP approval
4. **Travel:** Pre-approval required

## Common Mistakes

### Documentation Errors

| Mistake | Impact | Prevention |
|---------|--------|------------|
| Missing receipt | Delay/denial | Photo immediately |
| Wrong category | Tax issues | Review category list |
| Incomplete info | Rejection | Use checklist |
| Missing purpose | Audit risk | Note always |

### Policy Violations

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Late submission | Delayed reimbursement |
| Over limit | Partial reimbursement |
| Personal expense | Non-reimbursement |
| Missing approval | Rejection |

### How to Fix

1. **Missing receipt:** Request duplicate or get memo approved
2. **Wrong category:** Correct before submission
3. **Over budget:** Get exception approval in advance
4. **Late submission:** Explain delay, get approval

## Currency & International

### Foreign Currency

- Record original currency and amount
- Note exchange rate used
- Use corporate card when possible
- Convert at time of expense

### International Per Diem

Check GSA rates for:
- Meals & Incidental Expenses (M&IE)
- Lodging rates by city
- First/last day adjustments

## Audit Preparation

### Keep These Records

- All receipts (digital or physical)
- Expense reports with approvals
- Travel itineraries
- Calendar showing meetings
- Business purpose documentation

### Retention Period

- Tax records: 7 years
- Reimbursement records: 3 years
- Receipt copies: 7 years

### Audit Response

1. Gather all documentation
2. Reconcile against reports
3. Prepare explanations
4. Provide promptly
5. Follow up as needed
