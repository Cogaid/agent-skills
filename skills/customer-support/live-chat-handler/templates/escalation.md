# Chat Escalation Templates

## When to Escalate

### Immediate Escalation Required
- [ ] Customer mentions legal action
- [ ] Security breach or data concern
- [ ] Customer mentions self-harm
- [ ] VIP/Enterprise account with complex issue
- [ ] System access beyond your permissions
- [ ] Customer explicitly requests supervisor (after resolution attempt)

### Consider Escalation
- [ ] Issue unresolved after 15+ minutes
- [ ] Customer frustration escalating despite efforts
- [ ] Technical issue requiring engineering
- [ ] Policy exception needed
- [ ] Same issue reported 3+ times

## Escalation Message Templates

### To Supervisor/Manager

**Template:**
```
Hi [Supervisor],

Escalating: [Customer Name] - [Company if B2B]
Account: [Account ID/Email]
Chat Duration: [Time]

ISSUE:
[Brief description of the problem]

ATTEMPTED:
• [Action 1]
• [Action 2]
• [Action 3]

CUSTOMER STATE:
[Calm/Frustrated/Angry] - [Any specific concerns]

NEEDS:
[What you need from supervisor - approval, takeover, guidance]

[Your Name]
```

### To Technical Team

**Template:**
```
TECH ESCALATION

Customer: [Name/Email]
Account: [ID]
Priority: [High/Medium/Low]

ISSUE:
[Technical description]

ENVIRONMENT:
• Device: [Device]
• Browser: [Browser/Version]
• OS: [Operating System]

STEPS TO REPRODUCE:
1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]

ERROR MESSAGES:
[Any error messages or codes]

CUSTOMER AVAILABILITY:
[When they can be contacted/are they still in chat]
```

## Customer Communication During Escalation

### Warm Transfer
```
I want to make sure you get the best possible help with this, [Name].

I'm connecting you with [Person/Team] who has the expertise to fully resolve this. Let me give them a quick summary so you won't need to repeat everything.

One moment please!
```

### Introducing the Specialist
```
[Name], I have [Specialist Name] here who will be taking over.

[Specialist Name], this is [Customer Name] who's experiencing [brief issue]. They've already tried [what was attempted].

I'll let [Specialist Name] take it from here - you're in great hands!
```

### Cold Transfer (Different Channel)
```
To fully resolve this, I need to connect you with our [Team] who work via [email/phone].

Here's what happens next:
1. I'm creating a priority ticket with all our conversation details
2. [Team] will review within [timeframe]
3. They'll reach out to you at [contact method]

You'll receive a confirmation email with ticket number [#]. Is [email] the best way to reach you?
```

### When Supervisor Takes Over
```
[Name], I have [Supervisor Name] joining us now.

[Supervisor], this is [Customer Name]. They're experiencing [issue summary] and [customer state - e.g., "understandably frustrated"].

[Supervisor Name] will take it from here, [Customer Name]. Thank you for your patience.
```

## Post-Escalation Templates

### Confirmation to Customer
```
Your issue has been escalated to our [Team/Specialist].

Here's your reference information:
• Ticket #: [Number]
• Assigned to: [Team/Person]
• Expected response: Within [timeframe]

Is [email/phone] the best way for them to reach you?
```

### If Customer Must Wait for Callback
```
I've escalated this to [Team] who will call you at [phone number].

Expected callback: Within [timeframe]

A few things while you wait:
• Keep your phone handy
• They may call from [number/blocked number]
• Have [account info/order number] ready

Is there anything else I can help with in the meantime?
```

### Follow-Up After Escalation Resolved
```
Hi [Name]!

I wanted to follow up on the issue we escalated on [date].

I see that [Team/Person] has [resolution]. Is everything working well now?

If you need anything else, I'm happy to help!
```

## Internal Escalation Notes

### Standard Format
```
===== ESCALATION NOTES =====
Date/Time: [Timestamp]
Agent: [Your Name]
Customer: [Customer Name/ID]

SUMMARY:
[2-3 sentence summary of issue and customer state]

CONVERSATION HIGHLIGHTS:
• [Key point 1]
• [Key point 2]
• [Key point 3]

ACTIONS TAKEN:
• [Action 1] - Result: [Result]
• [Action 2] - Result: [Result]

ESCALATION REASON:
[Why you're escalating]

RECOMMENDED ACTION:
[What you think should happen]

CUSTOMER EXPECTATIONS:
[What the customer is expecting]
============================
```

### For Angry Customer
```
⚠️ SENSITIVE ESCALATION ⚠️

Customer is [frustrated/angry/upset] regarding [issue].

TRIGGER:
[What caused the escalation in emotion]

CUSTOMER STATEMENTS:
• "[Direct quote if relevant]"
• "[Direct quote if relevant]"

DE-ESCALATION ATTEMPTED:
• [What you tried]
• [Response]

RECOMMENDATION:
[Suggested approach for next agent]

NOTE: Customer may need [compensation/extra care/specific solution].
```

## Escalation Tracking

### Log Format
```
| Timestamp | Customer | Issue Type | Escalated To | Reason | Resolution | Time to Resolve |
|-----------|----------|------------|--------------|--------|------------|-----------------|
| [Time]    | [Name]   | [Type]     | [Team/Person]| [Why]  | [Outcome]  | [Duration]      |
```

### Weekly Review Questions
1. What issues were escalated most frequently?
2. Which escalations could have been prevented?
3. What additional training/resources would help?
4. Are there process improvements needed?
