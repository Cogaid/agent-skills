## Example: Login Error

```markdown
# Can't log in: "Invalid credentials" error

Getting an "Invalid credentials" error when trying to log in? Here's how to fix it.

## Symptoms

You might see:
- "Invalid email or password" message
- Redirect back to login page after entering credentials
- Account appears locked after multiple attempts

## Quick solution

First, make sure you're using the correct email address. Check for typos and try again.

**Did that work?** Great! If not, try the solutions below.

## Other solutions

### Reset your password

Your password may have expired or been changed.

1. Go to [yourapp.com/login]
2. Click **Forgot password?**
3. Enter your email and click **Send Reset Link**
4. Check your email and follow the link
5. Create a new password and log in

### Check Caps Lock

Passwords are case-sensitive.

1. Look at your Caps Lock key
2. If it's on, turn it off
3. Retype your password carefully

### Clear saved passwords

Your browser might be auto-filling an old password.

1. Click the password field
2. Look for saved password suggestions
3. Delete any old entries
4. Type your password manually

### Check if your account is locked

After 5 failed attempts, accounts lock for 15 minutes.

1. Wait 15 minutes
2. Try logging in again
3. Use the correct password on the first attempt

## Still having trouble?

If you still can't log in:

1. Note the exact error message
2. Confirm the email address you're using
3. Contact support at support@yourapp.com

Include "Login issue" in your subject line for faster routing.

## Related articles

- [How to reset your password]
- [How to enable two-factor authentication]
- [How to update your email address]
```

## Writing Tips

1. **Title**: State the problem clearly
2. **Quick solution**: Lead with the most common fix
3. **Order solutions**: Most common → least common
4. **Be specific**: "Clear browser cache" not "Clear cache"
5. **Explain why**: Brief context helps users understand
6. **Escape hatch**: Always provide contact support option
