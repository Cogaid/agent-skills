## Escalation Levels

### Level 1 - Frustrated

**Indicators**:
- Expresses disappointment
- Multiple contacts on same issue
- Firm but professional tone
- "This is frustrating" language

**Typical causes**:
- Delayed response
- Issue not resolved first time
- Unclear communication
- Workaround not working

**Response approach**:
- Extra attention and care
- Faster response time (target: 2 hours)
- Single point of contact
- Proactive updates

**Authority required**: Tier 1 Support

---

### Level 2 - Angry

**Indicators**:
- Raised voice or aggressive language
- Threats to cancel subscription
- Demands to speak with manager
- Multiple exclamation marks, ALL CAPS
- "Unacceptable" or "ridiculous" language

**Typical causes**:
- Repeated failures
- Broken promises
- Significant business impact
- Long resolution time

**Response approach**:
- Active listening (let them vent)
- Sincere, specific apology
- Immediate escalation to senior agent
- Concrete action with timeline
- Follow-up within 24 hours

**Authority required**: Tier 2 Support or Team Lead

---

### Level 3 - Critical

**Indicators**:
- Legal language ("lawyer," "lawsuit")
- Regulatory threats ("GDPR," "compliance")
- Media/social media threats
- C-level involvement at customer company
- Formal written complaints
- Multiple escalation attempts

**Typical causes**:
- Severe service failure
- Data breach or security incident
- Significant financial loss
- Compliance violation
- Repeated escalation failures

**Response approach**:
- Immediate senior leadership involvement
- Legal/PR awareness notification
- White-glove treatment
- Personal executive outreach if warranted
- Full documentation for legal team

**Authority required**: Manager + Legal/Compliance notification

## Compensation Guidelines

### Standard Compensation Matrix

| Impact Level | Free Tier | Pro Tier | Enterprise |
|--------------|-----------|----------|------------|
| Minor inconvenience | Apology only | Apology | Apology + goodwill credit |
| Service disruption (< 4 hours) | N/A | 1 week credit | 1 month credit |
| Service disruption (4-24 hours) | N/A | 2 weeks credit | 1 month + features |
| Major issue (> 24 hours) | N/A | 1 month credit | 2 months + exec call |
| Severe/data loss | Case-by-case | Case-by-case | Custom package |

### Compensation Decision Tree

```
1. Was this our fault?
   └── No → Apology only, help resolve
   └── Yes → Continue to 2

2. What was the business impact?
   └── None/minimal → Sincere apology + goodwill gesture
   └── Moderate → Standard compensation per matrix
   └── Severe → Manager approval for custom package

3. What is customer tier?
   └── Free → Limited options, focus on resolution
   └── Pro → Standard matrix applies
   └── Enterprise → Enhanced compensation + relationship management
```

### Compensation Types

| Type | When to Use | Approval Level |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Apology only** | Minor issues, quick resolution | Agent |
| **Service credit (< 1 month)** | Standard service issues | Agent |
| **Service credit (1-3 months)** | Significant issues | Team Lead |
| **Feature upgrades** | Extended issues, retention risk | Manager |
| **Contract modifications** | Enterprise relationships | Director |
| **Refunds** | Extreme cases, failed service | Finance + Manager |

### Compensation Don'ts

- Never offer compensation before acknowledging the issue
- Don't lead with the largest offer
- Don't promise what you can't deliver
- Don't set precedents you can't maintain
- Document all compensation with justification

## Legal Triggers

### Immediate Legal Team Notification Required

**Keywords requiring escalation**:
```
lawyer, attorney, lawsuit, sue, legal action, court,
litigation, breach of contract, damages, negligence,
liable, liability, terms violation, statutory rights
```

**Regulatory keywords**:
```
GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, PCI, SOX, compliance violation,
data protection authority, ICO, FTC complaint,
regulatory body, audit, fine, penalty
```

### Response Protocol for Legal Threats

1. **Do NOT**:
   - Admit fault or liability
   - Make promises about resolution
   - Discuss specific legal matters
   - Argue or become defensive

2. **DO**:
   - Remain calm and professional
   - Acknowledge their concern
   - Take detailed notes
   - Escalate immediately to legal team
   - Provide legal team contact if requested

**Response template**:
```
I understand you have serious concerns. I want to make sure
these are handled appropriately. I'm escalating this to our
legal team who can address this properly. You'll hear from
them within [timeframe].

Is there anything else related to your day-to-day use of
our service I can help with in the meantime?
```

## De-escalation Techniques

### Active Listening

| Technique | Example Phrases |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Verbal acknowledgment | "I see," "I understand," "Go on" |
| Summarizing | "So what I'm hearing is..." |
| Clarifying | "Help me understand..." |
| Silence | [Pause 3 seconds after they finish] |

### Empathy Statements

**Use**:
- "I understand why you're frustrated"
- "That sounds incredibly frustrating"
- "I'd be upset too if this happened to me"
- "You have every right to be disappointed"

**Avoid**:
- "I'm sorry you feel that way" (dismissive)
- "Our policy is..." (deflecting)
- "Actually..." (argumentative)
- "But..." (negating)

### Ownership Language

**Use**:
- "I'm going to personally handle this"
- "I take full responsibility for getting this resolved"
- "Here's exactly what I'm doing right now"

**Avoid**:
- "That's another department"
- "I wasn't the one who..."
- "Our system doesn't allow..."

### Recovery Statements

**After resolution**:
- "Thank you for your patience"
- "I appreciate you giving us the chance to make this right"
- "Your feedback helps us improve"

## Documentation Requirements

### Escalation Log Entry

Every escalation must be documented with:

```
ESCALATION LOG
--------------
Date: [Date/Time]
Ticket ID: [ID]
Customer: [Name/Account]
Tier: [Free/Pro/Enterprise]

ESCALATION LEVEL: [1/2/3]
TRIGGER: [What caused escalation]

CUSTOMER JOURNEY:
- [Date]: [First contact summary]
- [Date]: [Subsequent contacts]
- [Date]: [Escalation trigger event]

ROOT CAUSE:
[What actually went wrong]

RESOLUTION:
[Actions taken]

COMPENSATION PROVIDED:
[Details and justification]

FOLLOW-UP REQUIRED:
[Y/N - If yes, what and when]

SYSTEMIC ISSUES:
[Any process/product improvements needed]

CUSTOMER SENTIMENT AT CLOSE:
[Satisfied/Neutral/Unsatisfied]
```

### Required for Level 2+

- Manager notification
- CRM escalation flag
- If Enterprise: Success Manager notification

### Required for Level 3

- All of the above PLUS:
- Legal team notification
- Executive summary within 24 hours
- Post-mortem within 1 week
